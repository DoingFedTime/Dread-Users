I bought my dream car in 1991 and watched it go to the wreckers on a tow truck a couple of days ago.
by /u/Beelzebub snⱯ ɯoɹɟ ɐɓuᴉN • 5 months ago in /d/CafeDread
I've been a member of five car clubs since it got it, and have participated in motorsport events with all of them. I was the second longest-standing member and club secretary at one of them. What better birthday present could I buy myself than a day racing? I'd guess I've spent half of my birthdays at the track in the last 30 years.

I bought a standard car and started modifying it after a year. By 1998, it was making the most horsepower at the club dyno days. By 2000, everything you couldn't see had been replaced by race spec parts. It always looked like a standard car.

I've never wanted another car in all that time. I'd make it go faster instead of trading it in for a new car every few years.

I always knew I'd have to give up my childhood toys and join the grown ups eventually. I got to drive my race car to apply for the pension. I suppose that's the best I could hope for.

I couldn't get parts. I'd been sourcing them for mechanics from the US for years, and the company I was getting them from had shut down.

Second gear was dying. I'd had the gearbox reconditioned, but I couldn't get the part for second. The electric window controls were half broken, and I couldn't find a replacement. The air conditioning solenoid died a few years ago and there was no replacement part.

They sold less than 1,000 cars in Australia. Rare old vehicles are hard to keep running.
